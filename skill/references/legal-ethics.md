# Israeli Law — Geriatrics Shlav A Reference

## חוק החולה הנוטה למות, תשס"ו-2005 (Dying Patient Act)

### Who qualifies as "dying patient" (חולה הנוטה למות)
- Life expectancy < 6 months
- Must be confirmed by **3-physician committee** (ועדה מוסדית)
- Committee includes: attending physician + 2 others, including one specialist

### Patient rights under the law
- Can refuse ANY life-prolonging treatment including:
  - Mechanical ventilation
  - Dialysis
  - Artificial nutrition (NG tube, PEG, TPN)
  - CPR
  - Antibiotics (if goal is prolongation)
- Refusal is binding even if patient loses capacity AFTER documented refusal

### Living Will (הנחיות מקדימות)
- Legally binding advance directive
- Overrides family wishes
- Does NOT require notary — requires 2 witnesses + physician signature
- Registered with national registry (Ministry of Health)
- Can be for specific treatments OR general (palliative only)
- Revocable at any time by patient with capacity

### Withdrawal vs Withholding
- **Withholding** (אי-התחלת טיפול): Permitted based on patient/proxy refusal
- **Withdrawal** (הפסקת טיפול): More complex. Law allows withdrawal IF:
  - Continuous treatment (e.g. ventilation): requires committee
  - Cyclic treatment (e.g. dialysis, chemotherapy): patient/proxy can refuse next cycle
  - **Key exam point:** Ventilator = continuous → harder to withdraw. Dialysis = cyclic → can refuse.

### Palliative sedation
- Permitted. Not considered euthanasia.
- Intent = comfort, not to hasten death

### Physician duties
- Cannot withdraw treatment unilaterally
- Must provide information for informed decision
- Cannot force treatment on capacitated patient who refuses

## Patient Rights Law (חוק זכויות החולה, 1996)

### Core rights
1. **Right to treatment** — not denied on discriminatory grounds
2. **Informed consent** — full disclosure before any procedure; written for surgery/invasive
3. **Right to information** — diagnosis, prognosis, alternatives; can also choose NOT to know
4. **Second opinion** — right to request, hospital must facilitate
5. **Confidentiality** — medical information private; can be disclosed only with consent
6. **Dignity and privacy** — physical and personal
7. **Complaint mechanism** — rights commissioner (נציב קבילות) in every hospital

### Proxy decision-making hierarchy (incapacitated patient)
1. Legal guardian (אפוטרופוס) appointed for medical decisions
2. Continuing POA holder (מיופה כוח לפי ייפוי כוח מתמשך)
3. Spouse
4. Adult children
5. Parents
6. Adult siblings
If none: attending physician + ethics committee

## ייפוי כוח מתמשך (Continuing Power of Attorney, 2016)

### What it covers
- **Medical decisions** (healthcare proxy)
- **Financial decisions** (property management)
- **Personal decisions** (residence, daily life)
- Can appoint different proxies for each domain

### Requirements
- Person must have **legal capacity** (כשרות) at time of appointment
- Once diagnosed with dementia → **cannot** appoint POA (too late)
- Must be prepared with **notary** (עורך דין/נוטריון)
- Registered with registrar of the Ministry of Justice
- Becomes effective **only when person loses capacity** (not before)

### Exam distinctions
- POA ≠ Guardianship: POA is chosen by person; guardianship is court-imposed
- Continuing POA supersedes guardianship if properly registered
- Can specify conditions: "only if 2 physicians confirm I lack capacity"

## Guardianship (אפוטרופסות) — Guardianship Law 1962

### Types
- **Guardian of the person** (אפוטרופוס לגוף) — medical, personal decisions
- **Guardian of property** (אפוטרופוס לרכוש) — financial decisions
- **Both** — comprehensive

### Key principles (Capacity Law 1962 + 2016 amendment)
- **Limited guardianship preferred** — only what person cannot manage (חוק הכשרות המשפטית)
- **Full guardianship = absolute last resort**
- Court must find: person lacks capacity AND no less restrictive alternative
- Guardian must act in best interest, consult patient as much as possible

### Process
- Application to family court (בית משפט לענייני משפחה)
- Medical opinion required (psychiatric or neurological assessment)
- Guardian can be: family member, attorney, welfare officer

## כשרות vs כשירות — Exam Critical Distinction

| Term | Hebrew | Who determines | Standard |
|------|--------|----------------|----------|
| Legal competency | כשרות משפטית | **Court** | Presumed intact; needs court ruling to remove |
| Clinical capacity | כשירות קלינית | **Physician** | Task-specific, fluctuating, assessed per decision |

**Exam trap:** "The patient is incompetent" — this is a LEGAL statement. Physician assesses **capacity** not **competency**.

Capacity assessment: patient understands → appreciates consequences → reasons → communicates choice.
Capacity is decision-specific (can have capacity for some decisions, not others).

## Elder Abuse (התעללות בקשיש)

### Types (EXAM: all 5 types)
1. Physical (גופנית)
2. Psychological/emotional (נפשית/רגשית)
3. Financial/economic (כלכלית)
4. Sexual (מינית)
5. Neglect (הזנחה) — most common in community

### Mandatory reporting
- Healthcare workers in Israel: **mandatory reporters** for elder abuse suspected in institution
- Report to welfare services (שירותי רווחה)
- Patient confidentiality does NOT override reporting duty for abuse

## Fitness to Drive (כשירות לנהיגה)

- Physician has duty to ADVISE patient — not legal duty to report in Israel (unlike some countries)
- If high risk: recommend voluntary cessation; involve family
- Conditions requiring assessment: dementia, seizures, syncope, vision impairment, severe sleep apnea
- Dementia: driving assessment by occupational therapist (הערכת נהיגה)
- MMSE <24 = significant impairment; may not drive without formal assessment
- Physician documents recommendation; patient responsibility to comply
