## FREQUENTLY REPEATED STEM PATTERNS

### 1. Delirium after hip replacement surgery (appears 4+ times across years)
**Pattern:** Elderly patient, night after THA/fracture repair, confusion.
- Correct: Early mobilization + non-pharm delirium prevention (NOT antipsychotics first)
- If CAM negative: question asking what to do next → mobilize
- Trap: Prescribing haloperidol as first step = WRONG

### 2. Advance Directives + Capacity conflict
**Critical exam nuance** (appeared multiple times):
- If patient has CURRENT CAPACITY: their current verbal wish OVERRIDES prior written directives
- Living will applies only when patient LACKS CAPACITY
- Pattern: Patient with advance directives now verbally requesting something different → follow current wish if capacitated
- Trap: Blindly following old written directive when patient is cognitively intact

### 3. POA activation
**Pattern:** Patient with dementia, has continuing POA, needs procedure.
- Correct answer: Activate POA → get proxy consent
- Trap: Going to court first (guardianship) when POA already exists

### 4. הנחיות מקדימות + family opposition
- Patient directive is BINDING even if family objects
- Physician must follow patient's directive, not family preference

### 5. CAM-positive patient + treatment question
- First line = non-pharmacological (reorientation, hydration, mobilization, family presence)
- Never: BZD (worsens delirium), anticholinergics, high-dose antipsychotics
- Haloperidol if severely agitated: lowest effective dose, short course, document rationale

### 6. Frailty Fried criteria question
- Usually presented as: "patient has X of 5 criteria" → classify
- ≥3 = frail, 1-2 = pre-frail, 0 = robust
- Frailty ≠ disability ≠ multimorbidity → examined as distinct concepts

### 7. HFpEF treatment
- Stem: elderly woman with EF 55-65%, dyspnea, bilateral edema
- Correct: SGLT2i (empagliflozin/dapagliflozin)
- Traps: "digoxin reduces mortality" (WRONG — only hospitalizations), "ACEi proven in HFpEF" (WRONG)

### 8. SGLT2 in elderly diabetic with HF
- SGLT2i reduce CV death + HF hospitalization regardless of diabetes status
- Also reduce CKD progression (DAPA-CKD, CREDENCE)

### 9. Nutrition — MNA vs albumin
- MNA-SF = correct screening tool for geriatric malnutrition
- Albumin = NOT a good nutritional marker (negative acute phase reactant)
- Prealbumin = better but also affected by inflammation
- CRP high → all protein biomarkers unreliable

### 10. Beers drug identification
- Question gives drug, asks "is this appropriate?"
- Diphenhydramine in elderly for sleep = BEERS, WRONG
- Oxybutynin oral in elderly with urgency incontinence = BEERS → switch to mirabegron
- Nitrofurantoin with CrCl<30 = CONTRAINDICATED (ineffective + toxic)

### 11. Falls assessment — what to do
- Correct: Comprehensive multifactorial assessment (not single intervention)
- Include: medications review, vision, gait/balance, home hazards, orthostatism
- Vitamin D supplementation = part of all falls prevention programs

### 12. Dementia driving
- MMSE <24 = formal driving assessment required (not automatic ban)
- Inform patient, document. Not mandatory to report to DMV in Israel.
- Patient has responsibility to comply with physician advice.

## ISRAELI LAW QUESTION PATTERNS

### Most common legal scenario types:
1. **Capacitated patient refuses treatment** → Always respect refusal (Patient Rights Law)
2. **Advance directive vs current family wish** → Directive wins (if no capacity)
3. **Advance directive vs current patient capacity** → Current patient wins
4. **Patient lacks capacity, no POA, no guardian** → Proxy hierarchy → ethics committee
5. **Is this patient dying?** → Needs 3-physician committee + <6 months prognosis
6. **Withdrawal of cyclic treatment** → Patient/proxy can refuse next cycle (easier than continuous)
7. **Withdrawal of ventilator** → Requires committee (continuous treatment)
